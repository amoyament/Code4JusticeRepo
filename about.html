Our Impact

Our organization has been serving the Charlotte area since 2018. During that time, we've helped over 400 ex-convicts find gainful employment, reducing recidivism rates in the area by an estimated 15%. We work closely with local businesses to provide job training and placement services that meet their specific needs. Our partnerships with companies such as Yadkin Bank and Duke Energy have resulted in job placements in fields ranging from construction to finance, with starting salaries averaging around $35,000 per year. Our program has a success rate of over 80%, meaning that the vast majority of our clients are able to maintain steady employment for at least a year after placement. We believe that everyone deserves a second chance, and our impact on the Charlotte area is evidence that our approach is working.

Success Stories


John Henke

John was released from prison after serving a 5-year sentence for drug-related charges. With a criminal record and no job experience, he struggled to find employment and fell back into old habits. He came to Re|Con and received job training and placement assistance. He was able to secure a job as a line cook at a local restaurant, and has been sober and employed for over a year.


Aimee Fitzgerald

Aimee was convicted of embezzlement and served a 2-year sentence. She was determined to turn her life around and approached our organization for help. She received job training and was placed in a customer service position at a call center. Within a year, she was promoted to a management position, and has been praised by her supervisors for her work ethic and professionalism.


David Talley

David had been in and out of prison for most of his adult life. He was released from prison for the last time at the age of 45, and had no job prospects and no support system. He came to us for help and received job training in the construction industry. He was placed in a position as a construction laborer, and has since moved up to a foreman position. He recently celebrated his 5-year anniversary with the company and is now a mentor to other ex-convicts in our program.
